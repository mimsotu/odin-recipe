Apricot and Almond Crumble

Recipe credit: Justine Pattison for BBC Food.

Did you know?

Stop and smell the… apricots? Believe it or not, apricots are a member of the rose family.

Ingredients

  • 2 x 420g cans apricot halves in juice or syrup
  • 100g marzipan
  • 50g plain flour
  • 50g porridge oats (not jumbo)
  • 50g demerara sugar
  • 2 tbsp sunflower oil or 25g butter
  • 15g flaked almonds (optional)
  • 400g tin custard

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 200 C / 180 C Fan / Gas 6. Drain the apricots, reserving the juice. Put the apricot halves in a shallow 1 litre ovenproof dish. Pour over 5 tablespoons of the reserved juice. Coarsely grate the marzipan on top and lightly toss.
  2. In a large bowl, rub the flour, oats, sugar and oil together until well combined. Stir in the flaked almonds (if using). Sprinkle the crumble mixture on top of the apricots.
  3. Bake for 30–35 minutes, or until the crumble topping is golden-brown and the filling is bubbling up around the sides.
  4. Warm the custard in a small saucepan and serve alongside the crumble.
