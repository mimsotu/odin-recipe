Rhubarb Crumble Recipe

Recipe credit: James Martin for BBC Food

Did you know?

Rhubarb is full of fibre to help your tummy digest delicious crumble, as well as calcium and vitamin K to keep your bones and blood healthy. But don’t eat the leaves - they’re poisonous!

Ingredients

  • 10 sticks of rhubarb
  • 8 tbsp caster sugar
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 110g butter, softened
  • 110g demerara sugar
  • 200g plain flour

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 180 C / 160 C Fan / Gas 4.
  2. Cut the rhubarb into 7½ cm long sticks and place on an oven tray. Sprinkle with 4 tablespoons of water and the caster sugar. Roast for 10 minutes. Sprinkle over the ginger and mix well.
  3. Fill an ovenproof dish about 4 cm deep with the rhubarb.
  4. Rub the butter into the flour then mix in the demerara sugar to make the crumble topping. Sprinkle over the rhubarb and bake for 35-45 minutes, or until the crumble topping is crisp and golden-brown and the rhubarb filling has softened and is bubbling.
  5. Allow to cool slightly before serving with double cream.
