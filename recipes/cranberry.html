Cranberry Crumble Bars

Recipe credit: Nadiya Hussain for Happy Foodie

Did you know?

Only 5% of cranberries are sold fresh - the rest get squished into cranberry sauce (for your Christmas dinner) or cranberry juice (for your UTI).

Ingredients

For the batter...

  • 375g plain flour
  • 200g caster sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 230g unsalted butter, plus extra for greasing the tin
  • 1 medium egg
  • 60ml whole milk
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

For the filling...

  • 150g frozen cranberries
  • 10g caster sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornflour
  • 1 lime, zest and juice

For the icing...

  • 120g icing sugar
  • 2 tbsp lime juice

Steps

  1. Start by greasing and lining a 23cm square tin and preheating the oven to 190°C/fan 170°C.
  2. Put the flour in a bowl with the caster sugar, baking powder and salt. Mix together. Add the butter and crumble in using your fingers until there are no lumps.
  3. Make a well in the centre, pour the egg, milk and vanilla into it and bring the dough together.
  4. Take a third of the dough and set aside. Take the remaining two-thirds of the dough and push into the base of the tin till compact.
  5. To make the filling, mix the cranberries, caster sugar, cornflour, lime zest and juice together in a bowl, then pour over the biscuit base. Now take the rest of the biscuit base mix and crumble all over the cranberries. Bake for 45-50 minutes.
  6. Take out and leave to cool in the tin, then remove from the tin. Mix the icing sugar and lime juice together, drizzle the icing over and cut into squares to serve.
